# Customer Context — Joint Expeditionary Directorate (JED)

**Fictional — for example purposes only.**

## Mission
JED supports joint force units operating in expeditionary, contested, and disconnected environments. Primary customer set: SF, SEAL, MARSOC, CA/PSYOP units requiring AI capabilities in DDIL conditions.

## Current Problem
Existing commercial AI tooling (GPT, Claude, Gemini) requires persistent cloud connectivity. Military networks in forward operational environments cannot guarantee connectivity; units deploying into contested areas have no assured path to use these tools at the edge.

## Stakeholders
- **Decision Maker:** Director, JED (1-star equivalent)
- **Technical Lead:** Chief Engineer, Office of Emerging Capabilities
- **End Users:** Forward-deployed analyst teams (12-24 operators per unit)

## Hot Buttons
- **Disconnected operations demonstrated, not promised.** JED has been burned by vendors who claimed edge capability but required cloud for "model updates" or "usage telemetry."
- **Commercial-item basis.** JED prefers CSO over R&D contracting — they want working tech, not research.
- **Rapid adapter path.** Mission sets change quarterly. Solutions must support new mission specializations without long re-training cycles.
- **IL5 path.** Must have a credible path to IL5 or not waste JED's time.

## Budget Signal
Informal: Phase 1 (solution brief) is feasibility only, no funding. Phase 2 targets ~$2-5M per selected vendor for initial deployment on 10-50 endpoints. Long-term interest at ~$20-40M depending on fleet size.
