# RFI Section Patterns

RFI is **market research, not a bid**. The government is shaping a future acquisition. Answer questions concisely. Shape requirements toward your strengths without overselling.

**Absolute rules:**
- NO pricing. Not ROM, not line items, not even ballpark. Pricing in an RFI kills credibility.
- NO solution pitches. Keep it factual.
- DO answer every question (even with "not applicable").

## cover-letter (required)
**Purpose:** 1 page. State interest, company context, RFI reference.
**Structure:** Opening paragraph on interest → 2-3 sentences on company fit → willingness to engage further.
**Tone:** Professional, not salesy.

## company-identification (optional — may be part of cover letter)
**Purpose:** CAGE, UEI, NAICS, size status, POC.
**Structure:** Small data block at top of cover letter or after.

## question-by-question-response (required)
**Purpose:** Direct answers to each RFI question, in order, verbatim-quoted.
**Structure:** Quote each question → 2-5 sentence answer → reference supporting capability or experience if applicable.
**Pitfalls:**
- Don't combine questions into narrative flow. Evaluators expect one-to-one.
- Don't over-disclose proprietary approach. Answer the question asked, not the question you wish was asked.

## capability-summary (required)
**Purpose:** High-level capability fit for the future procurement.
**Structure:** 1-2 pages. What we do, where we've done it. No pitch, no theme statement, no win themes.
**Patterns:** NOT applicable — this is not a proposal.

## assumptions-and-clarifications (optional)
**Purpose:** Flag ambiguities in the RFI that would shape a future RFP.
**Structure:** Numbered list of assumptions made in answering + questions we'd want clarified in an RFP.

## Global rules
- **No pricing.**
- **No commitments.**
- **No win themes, no ghosting, no discriminators** — those belong in competitive bids.
- **Every question answered** — even with "no experience in this area" or "not applicable to our business".
- **Short wins.** 5-page RFI responses routinely outperform 15-page ones.
