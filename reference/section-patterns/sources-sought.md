# Sources Sought Section Patterns

Sources Sought is market research to determine set-aside eligibility. The government is asking: are there enough qualified small businesses to set this aside for SB / 8(a) / SDVOSB / WOSB / HUBZone?

**Your job:** Prove you're qualified in the category that matters, and prove you can execute via relevant experience.

## cover-letter (optional)
**Purpose:** Short framing. 1 paragraph if included.

## company-identification (required)
**Purpose:** CAGE, UEI, NAICS, size status, small-biz category assertions (SDVOSB, WOSB, 8(a), HUBZone if applicable).
**Structure:** Data block — must include:
- Legal name
- CAGE + UEI
- Primary NAICS + size status under it
- Small-biz category assertions with certification numbers where applicable
- POC (name, email, phone)

## relevant-experience-table (required)
**Purpose:** Prove capability via prior contracts.
**Structure:** 3-5 row table:

| Contract # | Customer | Scope (1 sentence) | Value | Period | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Pitfalls:** Don't pad with barely-related work. 3 highly relevant beats 5 thin matches.

## capability-summary (required)
**Purpose:** Brief mapping of our capabilities to the stated requirement.
**Structure:** 2-3 paragraphs. What we do, where we've done it, how it maps to this Sources Sought.

## interest-and-teaming (optional)
**Purpose:** Signal interest in the future RFP + teaming preferences.
**Structure:** 1-2 sentences per bullet: plan to bid as prime / sub / both; any teammates already identified.

## Global rules
- **No pricing.**
- **No pitch.** This is a market research form, not a sales document.
- **Short wins.** 3-5 pages is ideal. Over 5 signals misunderstanding of the format.
- **NAICS and size status are the two most important items.** Get them right; the set-aside decision turns on them.
