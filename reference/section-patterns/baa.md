# BAA Section Patterns

BAAs are scientific peer review, not commercial evaluation. Lead with the hypothesis. The PI is the product.

## executive-summary (required)
**Purpose:** State the hypothesis, the approach, and the expected advance to the state of the art.
**Structure:** 1-page. Hypothesis → approach → novelty → expected outcome → team.
**Patterns:** 1 (theme statement on novelty)

## research-hypothesis (required)
**Purpose:** The scientific question this research answers.
**Structure:** 2-3 paragraphs. Background → gap in current state of the art → our hypothesis → why this hypothesis is tractable.
**Pitfalls:** Don't pitch a product. Pitch a question.

## technical-approach (required)
**Purpose:** How we'll test the hypothesis. The meat of the evaluation.
**Structure:** Decompose into 3-5 technical workstreams. Each workstream: methodology, expected intermediate result, risk + mitigation.
**Patterns:** 1, 2 (discriminator = novel methodology + proof-of-concept)

## work-plan-and-milestones (required)
**Purpose:** What gets produced when. Evaluators use this to judge feasibility.
**Structure:** Gantt-style table + milestone narrative per quarter.
**Reference graphic:** Typically a timeline figure with go/no-go gates marked.
**Patterns:** 3 (action caption asserting why the pacing is feasible)

## principal-investigator-bio (required)
**Purpose:** Merit review is personality-driven. The PI must be credible.
**Structure:** Narrative bio (1 page) + CV summary (publications, prior awards, current funding).
**Must include:** Prior awards in adjacent topics. Peer-reviewed publications relevant to the hypothesis.

## prior-research-results (optional but strongly recommended)
**Purpose:** Prove the PI/team has done this kind of work before.
**Include if:** Relevant published research or prior prototype work exists.
**Structure:** 2-4 paragraphs per cited prior result. What was tested, what was learned, why it informs this proposal.

## schedule-and-gates (required)
**Purpose:** Concrete timeline with decision points.
**Structure:** Table of phases with go/no-go criteria at each boundary.

## facilities-and-equipment (optional)
**Include if:** Specialized facilities or equipment are required. Describe access/availability.

## references (optional)
**Purpose:** Show familiarity with the state of the art.
**Include if:** The solicitation encourages or the hypothesis requires citation.

## Global rules
- **Hypothesis-first.** Never lead with capability.
- **Honest TRL claims.** Claiming TRL-7 when it's TRL-4 kills credibility with peer reviewers.
- **Cite peer-reviewed work.** Industry analyst quotes don't count.
- **Follow-on funding discussion** if the BAA is phased. Mention how results transition to next phase.
