# Adjectival Ratings — Standard Federal Source-Selection Scale

Five-level scale used across DoD and civilian source selections for Technical and Management factors. Derived from DoDI 5000.74, DFARS PGI 215.305, and the DoD Source Selection Procedures.

## The Scale

| Rating | Color | Meaning (short) |
|---|---|---|
| **Outstanding** | Blue | Exceeds requirements with significant strengths; low risk |
| **Good** | Purple | Exceeds requirements with at least one significant strength; low to moderate risk |
| **Acceptable** | Green | Meets requirements; no significant weaknesses; moderate risk |
| **Marginal** | Yellow | Does not clearly meet one or more requirements; significant weaknesses; high risk |
| **Unacceptable** | Red | Fails to meet requirements or has deficiencies; unawardable without major revision |

## Detailed Definitions

### Outstanding (Blue)
**Proposal meets requirements and indicates an exceptional approach and understanding of the requirements. Strengths far outweigh any weaknesses. Risk of unsuccessful performance is very low.**

A proposal earns Outstanding when:
- Multiple significant strengths are present across the factor
- Approach exceeds the PWS/SOW/SOO in substantive, not cosmetic, ways
- Evaluator can articulate specific benefits that reduce cost, schedule, or performance risk
- No significant weaknesses; any weaknesses are minor and do not offset strengths
- Evaluator confidence is high that the offeror will execute successfully

**What Gold Team looks for:**
- At least 2 Significant Strengths clearly documented in the draft
- Differentiators named and *proven* (proof points, not claims)
- Risk section demonstrates the offeror understands and has mitigated execution risk
- Win themes visible in every major section

### Good (Purple)
**Proposal meets requirements and indicates a thorough approach and understanding of the requirements. Proposal contains strengths which outweigh any weaknesses. Risk of unsuccessful performance is low to moderate.**

A proposal earns Good when:
- At least 1 Significant Strength is present
- Approach is above baseline compliance
- Strengths clearly outweigh any weaknesses
- Evaluator is confident in execution but can identify room for improvement

**What Gold Team looks for:**
- ≥1 Significant Strength clearly documented
- Compliance matrix shows all requirements Covered (not just Drafted)
- Win themes appear but may not be uniformly reinforced
- Discriminators stated but proof points may be thin in some sections

### Acceptable (Green)
**Proposal meets requirements and indicates an adequate approach and understanding of the requirements. Strengths and weaknesses are offsetting or will have little or no impact on contract performance. Risk of unsuccessful performance is moderate.**

A proposal earns Acceptable when:
- All requirements are met (compliance matrix: no Gaps, minimal Partials)
- Approach is baseline compliant but not differentiated
- Any weaknesses are offset by corresponding strengths
- Evaluator is neither impressed nor worried

**What Gold Team looks for:**
- Compliance matrix complete
- No Significant Weaknesses
- Approach reads as generic — could be any competent offeror
- Win themes absent or only in the executive summary
- This is the "passing grade" — winning proposals almost never stop here

### Marginal (Yellow)
**Proposal has not demonstrated an adequate approach and understanding of the requirements. Proposal has one or more weaknesses which are not offset by strengths. Risk of unsuccessful performance is high.**

A proposal earns Marginal when:
- One or more Significant Weaknesses are present
- Some requirements are only partially met (Partials in the compliance matrix)
- Approach has flaws the evaluator can articulate as execution risk
- Offeror may be asked to address weaknesses via discussions (if negotiated procurement) or may lose

**What Gold Team looks for:**
- ≥1 Significant Weakness
- Partial compliance rows in the matrix
- Execution risk present but possibly fixable through revision
- Gold Team's primary output: what specifically to fix to move to Acceptable, then Good

### Unacceptable (Red)
**Proposal does not meet requirements and contains one or more deficiencies. Proposal is unawardable.**

A proposal earns Unacceptable when:
- One or more Deficiencies (material failure to meet a requirement, or combination of weaknesses equivalent to a Deficiency) exist
- Cannot be corrected without major revision

**What Gold Team looks for:**
- Any Deficiency is an immediate Unacceptable until resolved
- Gap rows in the compliance matrix = Deficiency until assigned and drafted
- Must be fixed before submission — not debatable

## Rating the Factor (not individual sections)

A factor rating is derived from the pattern of Strengths and Weaknesses across all subfactors, not averaged. Presence of a single Deficiency → Unacceptable for that factor regardless of other strengths.

## Weighted vs. Unweighted Factors

Section M may state:
- **Equally weighted:** all factors have identical importance
- **Descending order of importance:** factor 1 > factor 2 > ... > factor N
- **Explicit weights:** factors with stated percentages (less common in FAR Part 15; more common in BAAs)

Gold Team output should match the solicitation's statement of weights. Do not invent percentages if the solicitation says "in descending order of importance."

## How Gold Team assigns a rating

1. Read the factor definition from Section M as captured in `working/proposal-plan.md`.
2. Read the relevant draft section(s) in `drafts/`.
3. Enumerate Strengths and Weaknesses using [strengths-weaknesses-deficiencies.md](strengths-weaknesses-deficiencies.md).
4. Determine whether any Strength rises to Significant Strength, and whether any Weakness rises to Significant Weakness or Deficiency.
5. Apply the scale above.
6. Document the rationale in the same style an evaluator would — cite specific paragraphs, tables, figures.
7. State **specifically** what would change the rating by one level up ("Moving to Good requires a Significant Strength in subfactor X; currently the draft has three Strengths but none rise to Significant because ...").
