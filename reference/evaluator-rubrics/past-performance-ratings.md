# Past Performance Confidence Ratings

Authority: FAR 15.305(a)(2), DoD Source Selection Procedures, CPARS rating scale. Past performance is rated differently from Technical/Management — using a confidence scale, not adjectival ratings.

## The Scale

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Substantial Confidence** | Based on the offeror's recent/relevant performance record, the Government has a high expectation that the offeror will successfully perform the required effort. |
| **Satisfactory Confidence** | Based on the offeror's recent/relevant performance record, the Government has a reasonable expectation that the offeror will successfully perform the required effort. |
| **Neutral Confidence** | No recent/relevant performance record is available or the offeror's performance record is so sparse that no meaningful confidence assessment rating can be reasonably assigned. |
| **Limited Confidence** | Based on the offeror's recent/relevant performance record, the Government has a low expectation that the offeror will successfully perform the required effort. |
| **No Confidence** | Based on the offeror's recent/relevant performance record, the Government has no expectation that the offeror will be able to successfully perform the required effort. |

## Neutral is not a penalty

Under FAR 15.305(a)(2)(iv), an offeror without a relevant performance history receives a **Neutral** rating and cannot be penalized for it. This protects new entrants. Gold Team should never rate a proposal **Limited** or **No Confidence** based on absence of past performance — that's Neutral.

## What makes past performance "recent and relevant"

**Recent:** Typically within 3-5 years of the solicitation, depending on how Section L defines it. Some solicitations require work currently ongoing.

**Relevant:** Similar in size, scope, complexity, and contract type to the current requirement. Weighed on:
- Dollar value (within an order of magnitude)
- Scope overlap (not just same customer)
- Complexity (similar technical difficulty)
- Contract type (FFP vs. CPFF performance history differs)

## How Gold Team rates past performance

1. Read the PP volume (`drafts/past-performance.md`) and the PP section of `proposal-plan.md` for how Section M defines relevance.
2. For each cited PP reference:
   - Is it Recent? (age vs. solicitation threshold)
   - Is it Relevant? (use the relevancy matrix below)
   - What's the CPARS rating or PPQ score?
3. Pattern-match to the scale:
   - **Substantial Confidence:** Multiple Very Relevant/Relevant with Satisfactory+ CPARS
   - **Satisfactory Confidence:** Mix of Relevant and Somewhat Relevant with Satisfactory+ CPARS
   - **Neutral Confidence:** No relevant record (new entrant, new technology area)
   - **Limited Confidence:** Relevant record exists but with Marginal CPARS or unresolved issues
   - **No Confidence:** Relevant record with Unsatisfactory CPARS or major performance issues

## Relevancy Matrix (typical)

| Relevancy | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Very Relevant | Effort involved essentially the same scope and magnitude of effort and complexities as this solicitation |
| Relevant | Effort involved similar scope and magnitude of effort and complexities this solicitation requires |
| Somewhat Relevant | Effort involved some of the scope and magnitude of effort and complexities this solicitation requires |
| Not Relevant | Effort involved little or none of the scope and magnitude of effort and complexities this solicitation requires |

Gold Team output should explicitly classify each cited PP reference at one of these four levels.

## Common Gold Team mistakes on PP

- **Rating a new entrant Limited instead of Neutral.** Absence of record = Neutral, not penalty.
- **Counting PP from a teammate as if it were the prime's.** Teammate PP is typically rated separately under "teaming" sub-criterion; solicitation specifies.
- **Ignoring CPARS ratings entirely.** A Relevant but Marginal CPARS still affects the overall confidence rating.
- **Over-claiming relevancy.** Evaluator will downgrade if claimed Very Relevant work turns out to be Somewhat Relevant on inspection.
