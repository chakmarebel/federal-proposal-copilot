# Price Evaluation — Gold Team Guidance

How price is evaluated in federal source selection, and what Gold Team should look for in the cost/pricing artifact.

## Evaluation methods

### Best Value Tradeoff (FAR 15.101-1)
Price is one of several factors; non-price factors are more important, equal to, or less important than price (stated in Section M). Evaluator explicitly trades higher price for higher technical/management/PP benefit.

**Gold Team check:**
- Is the price defensible for the proposed approach (cost realism)?
- Are the benefits asserted in the technical volume funded in the cost volume?
- Would a reasonable evaluator pay this premium over a lower-priced compliant offer? If yes — Significant Strength. If no — Weakness.

### Lowest Price Technically Acceptable (LPTA, FAR 15.101-2)
Award goes to the lowest-priced offer that meets all technical and other non-price requirements at an "Acceptable" level. No credit for being "better than Acceptable."

**Gold Team check:**
- Is the proposal over-engineered? Any Significant Strengths that add cost without changing the Acceptable/Unacceptable determination are waste.
- Is the price as low as it can credibly be? Cost realism protects against "buying in" but LPTA rewards sharpening the pencil.
- LPTA dominance: reduce labor mix, reduce travel, reduce indirect rate exposure.

### Cost Realism (FAR 15.404-1(d)) — Cost-Reimbursement contracts
For CPFF, CPIF, and similar — the Government estimates the "probable cost" to the Government based on realism of the offeror's proposed cost. Low proposed cost ≠ best offer if unrealistic.

**Gold Team check:**
- Are proposed hours sufficient for the technical scope? Under-bidding hours is the #1 cost-realism issue.
- Are indirect rates supported by NICRA or recent audit? Unsupported rates get adjusted upward.
- Are subcontract rates realistic and consistent with the work being passed through?

### Price Reasonableness (FAR 15.404-1(b)) — Fixed-Price contracts
Test: is the price fair and reasonable given market comparables, historical prices, and the IGCE?

**Gold Team check:**
- Price benchmarked against market / IGCE / prior similar awards
- Labor rates defensible
- Fee rate appropriate for risk profile

## Rating the Price factor

Federal source selection typically does NOT use adjectival ratings for price (no Outstanding/Good/Acceptable for price). Instead:

- **Best value tradeoff:** Price is a number, evaluator assesses it against non-price ratings
- **LPTA:** Pass/Fail on non-price, then ranked by price
- **Cost realism:** Proposed price vs. probable cost; delta flagged

Gold Team output for Price should therefore report:
- The proposed total
- An assessment of realism or reasonableness
- Specific risks (unsupported rates, under-bid hours, unrealistic subs, missing ODCs)
- Tradeoff assessment (if best-value): does the technical strength justify the price vs. likely competitors?

## Gold Team mistakes on Price

- **Rating price with adjectivals.** Don't. Price is a number and an assessment, not a rating.
- **Comparing to a competitor without data.** If you don't have an IGCE or competitor intel, say so. Don't fabricate a "price-to-win."
- **Ignoring cost realism on cost-reimbursement.** This is where most cost-reimbursement proposals lose.
- **Ignoring unbalanced pricing.** Shifting cost between periods / CLINs to game evaluation is flagged by modern source selection.
