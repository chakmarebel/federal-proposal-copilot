# ROM (Rough Order of Magnitude)

## Reader mindset
"Is this a $50K effort or a $5M effort? What changes the answer?" The reader uses this to decide whether to pursue, how to budget, or whether to write the RFP.

## Required artifacts
- Scope summary (one paragraph)
- ROM range (e.g., "$1.2M-$1.8M over 18 months" or "$850K ±30%")
- Basis of estimate (analogous project, parametric, expert judgment — name the method)
- Key assumptions (3-5 items, each with a cost-swing implication)
- Key risks to the estimate
- Validity window ("valid for 30 days pending requirements clarification")

## Common pitfalls
- Giving a point estimate without a range (you're signaling false precision)
- No assumptions list (the customer can't re-scope without them)
- Hidden labor categories (show the rough blend, not a DCAA buildup)
- Forgetting the validity window — ROMs rot fast
