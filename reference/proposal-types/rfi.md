# RFI Response

## Evaluator mindset
"What does industry say? What's feasible? What terms would make a future RFP competitive?" The government is gathering input, not selecting a vendor.

## Required artifacts
- Cover letter (one page, summarizing your interest)
- Q-by-Q response (answer each question verbatim in order)
- Company capability summary (not a pitch)
- Assumptions and clarifications
- Optional: suggested contract vehicle, NAICS, size considerations

## Common pitfalls
- Including pricing (kills credibility — shows you don't know what RFI means)
- Writing a pitch instead of answering questions
- Over-disclosing proprietary approach
- Not answering every question (even with "not applicable")
